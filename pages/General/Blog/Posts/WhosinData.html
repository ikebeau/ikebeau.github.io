Why is it so hard to keep defined roles?

Often times companies do not know who or what role they are seeking to hire. As the world of data is something that really only has been catching large attention over the last 2 decades many people still don’t know what these roles do and what seperates them from the rest.

When looking at job postings they often can deviate from the general idea of what each of these roles are because so much of their roles overlap one with another. In the world of Data you have to become as it where a Jack of all Data Trades specialist in one.

This is why I am seeking to explain what traditionally each is and how they difer from one another. This will not only help those that are curious but those that may be interested in Data but do not know what to pursue.

What are the roles

Truthfully there are so many different job titles that making a Blog post about them all would be an unproductive task. This is why I picked 10 to focus on who they are and what they generally do.

Data Scientist

Who Are Data Scientist?

Data Scientists focus on extracting insights and building predictive models utilizing both structured and unstructured data. They combine their expertise in statistics, machine learning, and domain knowledge to solve complex problems, and often times provide a simple way to retrieve future knowlegde from the model.

What Tools do they Use

  • Programming Languages:
    • Frequently: Python, R, and SQL
    • Sometimes: JavaScript, Html
  • Data Manipulation Libraries
    • Python: Pandas, Polars, PySpark, NumPy
    • R: Tidyverse RSpark, BaseR
  • Machine Learning
    • TensorFlow
    • PyTorch
    • Scikit-Learn
  • Visualization Libraries
    • Python: Plotly, MatPlotLib, Seaborn, Lets-Plot, Altair
    • R: Plotly, BaseR, Ggplot2
  • Big Data Tools:
    • Apache Spark
    • Hadoop
    • DataBricks
  • Cloud Platforms:
    • AWS
    • GCP
    • Azure

How do they use these tools?

Programming: - R & Python: For data cleaning, exploration, and modeling. - SQL: Data Retrieval from the Data Structure - JavaScript/Html: Webpage and App Developement after model completed

Machine Learning Frameworks: - To build, train, and evaluate predictive models.

Visualization Tools: - To communicate findings through charts and dashboards.

Big Data Tools: - To process and analyze large datasets efficiently.

Cloud Platforms: - To deploy and scale machine learning models.

How do they relate to the other roles? Why?

Data Analysts: Collaborate to interpret results and create actionable insights.

Data Engineers: Depend on their pipelines to access clean and organized data.

Machine Learning Engineers: Work together to deploy models into production.

Statisticians: Share knowledge of statistical methods for advanced analysis.

Data Analyst

What are Data Analysts

Data Analysts focus on interpreting data to provide actionable insights for business decisions. Data Analyst often will use structured data to create reports and dashboards

What tools do they use?

  • Spreadsheets: Excel, Google Sheets
  • BI Tools: Power BI, Tableau, Looker
  • SQL
  • Visualization: Power BI, Tableau, Matplotlib

How are the tools used

  • Spreadsheet
    • Lookup functions and Business Operations analytics often times
  • SQL
    • To extract and analyze data from the company relational database
  • BI Tools
    • Create Interactive Dashboards and Reports for the company
  • Visualization:
    • To present data trends and summarize to drive company decisions.

How do they relate to other roles?

Data Scientists: Data Analysts often will provide the data and general report to a Data Scientist so that they can further examine the data and create predictive models to help plan for the future

Data Engineers: Data Analysts rely on the Data Engineer to make sure the data is properly pipelined in a retrievable way for their analytics.

BI Developers: Data Analysts often work closely with BI Developers and can be mistaken as them because of their similar roles. However, they are seperate in that a BI Developer is solely in charge of report building where as the Data Analyst may be used for more than one project

Data Engineer

Who are Data Engineers?

Data Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure for data generation, storage, and access. The main purpose of a Data Engineer is the ensure the data is clean, reliable, and available for analysis.

What tools do they use?

Common Programming Languages: - Python - Java - Scala Data Pipelines: - Apache Airflow - Luigi - Azure Pipelines Big Data: - Hadoop - Apache Spark Cloud Platforms - AWS - Azure - GCP ETL Tools - Informatica - Talend

How do the tools get utilized

ETL: Meaning Extract, Transform and Load is the process for getting the data into a reuseable format.

Database Administrator (DBA)

Who are Database Administrators?

DBAs are responsible for managing and maintaining databases to ensure data is stored securely and efficiently. They are the ones who oversee who has access to what data. They also control who can overwrite, delete, and write data to the database.

What tools do they use?

Database Management Systems: - MySQL - PostgreSQL - Oracle - SQL Server - SSMS

Backup Tools: - RMAN - pgBackRest - SSMS - Backup Jobs

Monitoring Tools: - Nagios - SolarWinds - SSMS

How do they use these tools?

Database Management Systems: To create, manage, and optimize databases.

Backup Tools: Used to ensure data can be restored in case of failures.

Monitoring Tools: Used to track performance and resolve issues.

How do they relate to the other roles? Why?

Data Engineers: Data Admins work with Data Engineers to make sure pipelines are functional and efficent

Data Analysts: They make sure the Data Analyst has the data needed and only access to what is required for their job and analytic work.

Data Governance Analysts: Work together to ensure the data follows governement, industry, and company regulations.

Machine Learning Engineer

Who are Machine Learning Engineers?

Machine Learning Engineers specialize in deploying and optimizing machine learning models in production environments.

What tools do they use?

Programming Languages: - Python - Java

Machine Learning Frameworks: - TensorFlow - PyTorch

Deployment Tools: -Docker - Kubernetes

Cloud Platforms: - AWS SageMaker, - GCP AI Platform

How do they use these tools?

Machine Learning Frameworks: To train and fine-tune models.

Deployment Tools: To package and deploy models as APIs.

Cloud Platforms: To scale models for real-world applications.

How do they relate to the other roles? Why?

Data Scientists: Often times work together. In some cases the they will take the model the Data Scientist has created and recreate it within a production enviroment

Data Engineers: Works with the Data Engineer to ensure that the Pipeline is efficent and providing real-time or consistent data for the model.

AI Researchers: All AI is is Machine Learning on Steroids so they work together rather frequently

Data Governance Analyst

Who are Data Governance Analysts?

Data Governance Analysts ensure data is managed ethically, securely, and in compliance with regulations. They consistently look over laws such as HIPPA, COPPA, GLBA, FTC Act, FCRA, and state specific laws. Not only do they make sure government regulations are met, but company and industry.

What tools do they use?

Data Catalog Tools: - Collibra - Alation

Compliance Tools: - OneTrust - TrustArc

Metadata Management: - Apache Atlas

How do they use these tools?

Data Catalog Tools: To document and manage data assets.

Compliance Tools: To ensure adherence to data privacy laws.

Metadata Management: To track data lineage and quality.

How do they relate to the other roles? Why?

Database Administrators: Collaborate on data security and compliance.

Data Engineers: Ensure pipelines meet governance standards.

BI Developers: Help maintain data quality in dashboards.

Business Intelligence (BI) Developer

Who are BI Developers?

BI Developers design and implement dashboards and reports for business decision-making.

What tools do they use?

BI Tools: - Tableau - Power BI - Looker

SQL: For querying data

ETL Tools: - Talend - Informatica

How do they use these tools?

BI Tools: To create dashboards and reports.

SQL: To extract and transform data for visualization.

ETL Tools: To prepare data for analysis.

How do they relate to the other roles? Why?

Data Analysts: Use their dashboards for business insights.

Data Engineers: Ensure pipelines provide clean data for BI tools.

Data Architects: Collaborate on data modeling for BI solutions.

Data Architect

Who are Data Architects?

Data Architects design the overall structure of the data system to ensure scalability and efficiency.

What tools do they use?

Data Modeling Tools: - ER/Studio - dbt

Database Systems: - Snowflake - Redshift

Cloud Platforms: - AWS - GCP - Azure

How do they use these tools?

Data Modeling Tools: To create schemas and optimize data storage.

Database Systems: To implement scalable architectures.

Cloud Platforms: To manage distributed data systems.

How do they relate to the other roles? Why?

Data Engineers: Implement the architectures Data Architects design.

DBAs: Maintain and optimize Data Architects designs.

BI Developers: Use Data Architects models for dashboards.

Statistician

Who are Statisticians?

Statisticians apply statistical methods to analyze and interpret data, often focusing on experimental design and hypothesis testing.

What tools do they use?

Statistical Software: - R - SAS - Python

Visualization Tools: - ggplot2 - Matplotlib - Plotly - BaseR

How do they use these tools?

R/SAS: For advanced statistical analysis.

Python: For data manipulation and modeling.

Visualization Tools: To present findings effectively.

How do they relate to the other roles? Why?

Data Scientists: Share expertise in statistical modeling.

Data Analysts: Often can produce visualizations that help the Statitician dertimine what to analyze through Statistical Modeling

AI Researchers: Collaborate on model evaluation and validation.

AI Researcher

Who are AI Researchers?

AI Researchers develop cutting-edge algorithms and models to advance the field of artificial intelligence.

What tools do they use?

Programming Languages: - Python - C++

Deep Learning Frameworks: - TensorFlow - PyTorch

Research Tools: - Jupyter Notebooks - LaTeX

How do they use these tools?

Deep Learning Frameworks: To create and train novel AI models.

Research Tools: To document and publish findings.

How do they relate to the other roles? Why?

Machine Learning Engineers: Implement their algorithms and models into production.

Data Scientists: Collaborate on experimental designs and exploratory analysis.

Statisticians: Leverage statistical methods for validating AI models.

Data Engineers: Provide infrastructure for large-scale data processing to train AI models.

import networkx as nx
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt


roles = [
    "Data Scientist", "Data Analyst", "Data Engineer", 
    "Database Administrator", "Machine Learning Engineer", 
    "Data Governance Analyst", "BI Developer", 
    "Data Architect", "Statistician", "AI Researcher"
]

relationships = [
    ("Data Scientist", "Data Analyst"),
    ("Data Scientist", "Data Engineer"),
    ("Data Scientist", "Machine Learning Engineer"),
    ("Data Scientist", "Statistician"),
    ("Data Scientist", "AI Researcher"),
    ("Data Analyst", "Data Engineer"),
    ("Data Analyst", "BI Developer"),
    ("Data Engineer", "Database Administrator"),
    ("Data Engineer", "Machine Learning Engineer"),
    ("Data Engineer", "BI Developer"),
    ("Data Engineer", "Data Architect"),
    ("Database Administrator", "Data Governance Analyst"),
    ("Database Administrator", "Data Engineer"),
    ("Machine Learning Engineer", "Data Scientist"),
    ("Machine Learning Engineer", "AI Researcher"),
    ("Machine Learning Engineer", "Data Engineer"),
    ("Data Governance Analyst", "Database Administrator"),
    ("Data Governance Analyst", "Data Engineer"),
    ("Data Governance Analyst", "BI Developer"),
    ("BI Developer", "Data Analyst"),
    ("BI Developer", "Data Engineer"),
    ("BI Developer", "Data Architect"),
    ("Data Architect", "Data Engineer"),
    ("Data Architect", "Database Administrator"),
    ("Data Architect", "BI Developer"),
    ("Statistician", "Data Scientist"),
    ("Statistician", "AI Researcher"),
    ("AI Researcher", "Data Scientist"),
    ("AI Researcher", "Machine Learning Engineer"),
    ("AI Researcher", "Statistician"),
    ("AI Researcher", "Data Engineer")
]

G = nx.DiGraph()

G.add_nodes_from(roles)
G.add_edges_from(relationships)

plt.figure(figsize=(12, 12))
pos = nx.spring_layout(G, seed=42)

nx.draw_networkx_nodes(G, pos, node_size=2000, node_color="lightblue")
nx.draw_networkx_edges(G, pos, edge_color="gray", arrowsize=20)
nx.draw_networkx_labels(G, pos, font_size=10, font_color="black", font_weight="bold")

plt.title("Relationships Between Data Roles", fontsize=16)
plt.axis("off")
plt.show()

The graph above though archeaic helps to begin to understand how each of these roles play into eachother visually. Each line shows that they share some major responsibilities or work together on tasks.

In conclusion, these roles overlap a lot with each other. In order for one to succeed they often need the other steps to do well or have a purpose. (Ex. a BI Developer cannot develop without a Data Engineer who cannot function without the architecture in place before hand.). However, it is important for each role to understand the process of the other and some of the vocabulary. I have learned this while working at Country Supplier. I have found that my knowledge in Data Engineering and Data Architecture though in some facets limited has in several cases helped our Data Engineer pipeline Data into Azure for us to make models and reports from. As stated before it is important to be a Jack of all trades and a Specialist in one. Until next time - Isaac
